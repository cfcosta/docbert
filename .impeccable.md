## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are solo developers and researchers using docbert’s local web UI on desktop while coding, researching, and working through their own notes, docs, and PDFs.

### Brand Personality

Dense, fast, technical. The interface should feel efficient and capable, but still deliberate rather than cramped or generic.

### Aesthetic Direction

Prioritize chat-first workflows, but present them through an editorial workspace lens rather than a generic dashboard. Favor strong reading flow, asymmetric composition, and document-oriented structure over repetitive app-card patterns.

### Design Principles

1. Put the conversation workspace first without turning the rest of the product into secondary clutter.
2. Use spacing rhythm to separate thinking, navigation, and reading zones.
3. Prefer editorial structure and alignment over generic dashboard cards.
4. Keep density high enough for expert users, but avoid cramped clusters and repetitive equal spacing.
5. Make search, documents, and settings feel like parts of one coherent research workspace.
